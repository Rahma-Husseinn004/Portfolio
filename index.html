About me

A student of the law, in practice as much as in principle.

“The law is reason, free from passion.” — Aristotle

I'm a third-year law student concentrating in constitutional and civil litigation, drawn to cases where careful argument meets real consequence. My coursework and clinics have given me a grounding in legal research, oral advocacy, and client counsel, but it's the hours spent preparing a brief until every citation earns its place that I find most satisfying.

Outside the classroom, I compete on the Moot Court team, contribute to the Law Review, and volunteer with a legal aid clinic serving low-income tenants. I'm looking toward a career in litigation, with a particular interest in appellate advocacy.
